What Drives Average Weekly Sales?
Collaborative Project · Tech Talent Pipeline Residency · Tableau

Collaborated with classmates to build a multi-view Tableau dashboard using the Walmart weekly sales dataset, testing which factors actually move retail sales: temperature, fuel price, CPI, unemployment rate, and the store itself. Each view lines up one variable against average weekly sales, plus a store performance matrix ranking every location from top performer to average.

The answer surprised us: store selection mattered far more than weather or the economy. The top-performing store did roughly 5x the average weekly sales of the bottom ones, while unemployment and fuel price barely moved the needle at all.

Limitations

No store locations, product categories, or customer demographics in the dataset, so we couldn't say why some stores outperform, only that they do.

What we'd try next

Layer in store location and product category data to explain the gap, and test dynamic pricing on temperature-sensitive product lines.
